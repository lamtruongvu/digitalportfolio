Power BI
Chocolate Sales Dashboard

Overview

A Power BI analysis of synthetic chocolate retail transaction data across 6 countries and 2 years (2023–2024), uncovering trends in sales performance, product preferences, and customer behaviour.

Problem

Chocolate retailers operating across multiple markets lack a clear, consolidated view of what's driving revenue, which products and channels perform best, and who their customers actually are. Without this, strategic decisions around marketing, product mix, and geographic expansion are made with limited evidence.

Approach

I explored a star-schema dataset from Kaggle spanning ~990K orders, then brainstormed questions across three dimensions (sales, product, and customer) before building three interconnected Power BI dashboards to surface the answers. Key focus areas included geographic performance gaps, channel and category preferences, and customer segmentation by age, gender, and loyalty.

Outcome

The dashboards revealed a highly stable but plateauing business with a consistent 40% profit margin, airport retail as the dominant sales channel (30%), and Praline as the top category - while identifying France as a quietly accelerating market and flagging the 80g pack and Milk chocolate category as potential candidates for rationalisation.

SQL
World's Forestation Analysis

Overview

A SQL-based investigation into global deforestation trends using World Bank data, analysing forest area changes across countries and regions between 1990 and 2016 to support ForestQuery's conservation mission.

Problem

Deforestation is a global crisis, but without a clear, data-driven picture of where forest loss is most severe - and where it's being reversed - conservation efforts and international funding can't be directed where they're needed most.

Approach

I queried three joined tables (forest area, land area, and regional classifications) using CTEs, window functions, and CASE-based quartile segmentation to answer questions at the global, regional, and country level - covering absolute loss, percentage change, forestation rankings, and income group breakdowns. I also refactored several queries for efficiency by eliminating redundant CTEs.

Outcome

The analysis found that the world lost 1.32M sq km of forest - roughly the size of Peru - between 1990 and 2016, with Brazil and Indonesia alone accounting for nearly two-thirds of global loss, while China bucked the trend with a gain of 527,229 sq km, making it the standout reforestation success story of the period.
